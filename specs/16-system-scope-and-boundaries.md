# System Scope and Boundaries

## Purpose

This document defines what belongs in the archive system, what does not, and why.

---

## Core Scope Definition

### This System Is For:

**1. Temporal Archives of Attention**
- What you noticed at a moment in time
- What you thought worth capturing
- What you were curious about or troubled by

**2. Rhizomatic Knowledge Graphs**
- Networked, non-hierarchical connections
- Concepts that recur across contexts
- Themes that emerge organically

**3. Reflective Diary Records**
- Emotional states and context
- Subjective experience
- Shifting perspectives over time

**4. External Material Integration**
- Articles, books, videos that prompted thought
- Wikipedia entries that provided context
- Conversations that sparked ideas

**5. Active Inquiry Lines**
- Questions being pursued
- Temporary lenses for organization
- Research-in-progress

---

## What Belongs in the Archive

### Definitely Include:

**Thoughts and Observations**
- Ideas that struck you
- Observations about patterns
- Hypotheses and speculations
- Uncertainty and confusion

**Emotional and Experiential Notes**
- How you felt in a moment
- Context around life circumstances
- Reactions to events or experiences
- Shifts in mood or energy

**Learning and Understanding**
- Concepts you're trying to grasp
- Connections you're making
- Questions you're exploring
- Things you don't understand yet

**Creative Work and Practice**
- Writing fragments
- Song ideas or lyrics
- Performance concepts
- Aesthetic observations

**External Material Responses**
- Reactions to articles you read
- Notes on books or videos
- Wikipedia entries that contextualized something
- Quotes that resonated

**Meta-Cognitive Observations**
- How you're thinking
- How your attention works
- What captures your focus
- How your interests shift

---

## What Does NOT Belong in the Archive

### Exclude:

**1. Pure Task Management**
- To-do lists for errands
- Shopping lists
- Appointment reminders
- Project deadlines without context

**Why**: This is not a productivity system. Temporal context (when something was important) matters; pure logistics don't.

**Exception**: If a task reflects something about your interests or concerns (e.g., "been procrastinating on this because it feels misaligned"), capture THAT, not the task itself.

---

**2. Routine Logs Without Reflection**
- Daily habit tracking
- Exercise logs
- Food diaries
- Sleep data

**Why**: These are quantified self-tracking, not thought archives.

**Exception**: If a pattern in these logs prompts reflection (e.g., "noticed I sleep worse when working late"), capture the OBSERVATION, not the raw data.

---

**3. Purely Functional Reference Material**
- API documentation
- Code snippets for reuse
- Command-line cheatsheets
- Configuration files

**Why**: This is a knowledge archive, not a technical wiki.

**Exception**: If reference material prompted a thought about how tools work or why design choices matter, capture THAT, with attribution.

---

**4. Other People's Content Without Your Response**
- Raw article copies with no commentary
- Bookmark dumps
- Screenshot archives without context
- Saved links without notes

**Why**: External material only matters when it intersects with your thinking.

**Exception**: If you capture something for future reference, add minimal context: "found this because I was thinking about X."

---

**5. Obligatory or Performative Captures**
- Notes taken because you "should" be interested
- Ideas captured to seem productive
- Concepts you don't actually care about
- Content captured for external validation

**Why**: This archive serves YOU, not an imagined audience.

**Exception**: If you notice you're capturing performatively, THAT observation belongs in the archive.

---

**6. Secrets That Must Stay Secret**
- Passwords or credentials
- Private information about others
- Legally sensitive material
- Content you'd be harmed by if exposed

**Why**: This system is Markdown files in a git repo—not built for secrets.

**Exception**: None. Use a proper secrets manager.

---

**7. Content You Don't Want Future-You to Read**
- Rants you'll regret
- Thoughts that would harm relationships if discovered
- Material that violates others' privacy
- Content that doesn't reflect your values

**Why**: The archive is long-term. Don't write what you can't live with later.

**Exception**: If uncertain, capture with caveat: "I'm feeling X right now; I may not feel this way later."

---

**8. Machine-Generated Output Without Interpretation**
- Raw AI-generated text dumps
- Automated summaries without review
- Transcription dumps without editing
- LLM outputs you didn't actually read

**Why**: This is YOUR archive of YOUR attention, not an AI's output.

**Exception**: If AI output prompted your thinking, capture YOUR RESPONSE to it.

---

## Boundary Cases (Include with Caution)

### 1. Work-Related Content

**Include when**:
- Work prompts genuine intellectual curiosity
- Work-related thinking connects to broader interests
- Work context explains emotional state
- Work problems reveal meta-patterns about systems

**Exclude when**:
- Content is purely professional obligation
- Material is company-confidential
- Capture is performative documentation
- Content has no personal resonance

**Guideline**: Capture what INTERESTS you about work, not work itself.

---

### 2. Social Media Content

**Include when**:
- A tweet prompts extended thinking
- A thread crystallizes something you've been pondering
- Online discourse reveals cultural patterns
- Social media interaction explains emotional context

**Exclude when**:
- You're just archiving tweets you liked
- Content is pure entertainment consumption
- Capture is compulsive scrolling documentation
- Material has no sustained interest

**Guideline**: Capture RESPONSES to social media, not the feed itself.

---

### 3. Personal Relationships

**Include when**:
- Conversations spark intellectual or emotional insights
- Relationship dynamics reveal patterns about yourself
- Interactions prompt reflection on values or identity
- Context explains other captures

**Exclude when**:
- Content violates others' privacy
- Material could harm relationships if discovered
- Capture is venting without insight
- Content is purely interpersonal logistics

**Guideline**: Capture what YOU learn about yourself, not detailed accounts of others.

---

### 4. Dream Logs

**Include when**:
- Dreams prompt interpretation or curiosity
- Dream content connects to waking themes
- Dreams reveal emotional states
- Dream symbols recur over time

**Exclude when**:
- Dreams are logged mechanically without interest
- Content is pure stream-of-consciousness
- Dreams have no thematic resonance
- Logging becomes obligatory ritual

**Guideline**: Capture dreams that MEAN something to you, not all dreams.

---

### 5. Financial or Material Concerns

**Include when**:
- Financial stress explains emotional context
- Economic concerns reflect broader anxieties
- Material circumstances shape your thinking
- Resource constraints affect your choices

**Exclude when**:
- Content is pure budgeting or accounting
- Material is sensitive financial data
- Capture is transaction logging
- Content has no reflective element

**Guideline**: Capture how circumstances FEEL and what they MEAN, not the details.

---

## Scope Evolution

### This Scope May Expand When:
- New capture surfaces emerge (e.g., wearables, voice)
- New interests develop (e.g., visual art, music)
- Life circumstances change (e.g., parenthood, relocation)
- Archive usage patterns reveal gaps

### This Scope Should NOT Expand To:
- Replace specialized tools (task managers, password managers)
- Become comprehensive life logging
- Document everything "just in case"
- Serve external audiences or purposes

---

## The "Does This Belong?" Test

### Ask:

**1. Does this reflect my attention or interest?**
- Yes → Probably include
- No → Probably exclude

**2. Will I care about this in 5 years?**
- Yes → Definitely include
- No → Probably exclude
- Unsure → Include (better to have than not)

**3. Does this have temporal context?**
- Yes → Definitely include
- No → Add context or exclude

**4. Does this prompt interpretation or questions?**
- Yes → Definitely include
- No → Probably exclude

**5. Am I capturing this FOR me or AT me?**
- For me → Include
- At me → Exclude

---

## Forbidden Scope Expansions

### Never Add:
- Automated life-logging without curation
- Social media mirrors (auto-import of all activity)
- Email archives (unless specific emails matter)
- Browser history dumps
- Location tracking logs
- Biometric data streams
- Transaction logs
- Calendar exports

**Why**: These create noise, not signal. They document activity, not attention.

---

## Success Criteria

This scope definition succeeds if:
- Users feel clear about what to capture
- Archive remains manageable over decades
- Signal-to-noise ratio stays high
- Captures feel personally meaningful
- Future self can read and understand

This scope definition fails if:
- Users feel uncertain about every capture
- Archive becomes overwhelming data dump
- Most notes are unused or uninteresting
- System serves obligation, not curiosity
- Future self can't find meaning in archive

---

## Summary

**Capture attention, not activity.**
**Capture meaning, not data.**
**Capture questions, not answers.**
**Capture YOUR thinking, not others' content.**

The archive is where your attention left traces—not where everything goes.
