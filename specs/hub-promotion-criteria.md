# Hub Promotion Criteria

## Purpose

This document defines when a concept should be promoted
from an incidental idea or repeated reference into a **hub note**.

Hub promotion is a structural decision.
It should be made sparingly and deliberately.

Hubs are discovered through use, not planned in advance.

---

## What a Hub Represents

A hub represents:
- a recurring conceptual place
- sustained gravitational pull across notes
- long-term relevance across time or contexts

A hub does NOT represent:
- a conclusion
- a summary
- a single interpretation
- a resolved idea

---

## Minimum Conditions for Hub Promotion

A concept may be promoted to a hub when **at least 2-3** of the following are true:

- The concept appears across multiple notes (3+)
- The notes occur at different times (span 3+ months)
- The concept is referenced in different contexts or moods
- The concept connects to multiple domains or themes
- The concept reduces wording drift when named consistently

**Quantified Guideline**: If a concept meets 2 of these criteria, promotion is *possible*. If it meets 3+, promotion is *recommended*.

No single condition is sufficient on its own.

**See also**: `specs/15-tags-and-hubs-interaction.md` for tag-to-hub promotion thresholds.

---

## Strong Indicators for Promotion

Promotion is especially appropriate when:

- Notes independently link to the same broad idea
- Slightly different phrasings repeatedly refer to the same concept
- The concept functions as a navigational shortcut
- The concept would benefit from holding contradictions
- The concept is likely to recur in future thinking

---

## Weak or Insufficient Indicators

Do NOT promote a hub when:

- The concept appears in only one note
- The concept is tied to a single project
- The concept is a phrased interpretation
- The concept is time-bound or situational
- The concept exists only to organize tags

---

## Naming Constraints

Promoted hubs must follow the hub naming rules:

- Noun or noun phrase
- Broad and non-judgmental
- No tense, metaphor, or emotional framing
- No implied conclusion

Good:
- Identity Formation
- Technology and Emotion
- Caregiving
- Electronic Music

Bad:
- Losing My Identity Online
- Why Technology Is Alienating
- The Hard Parts of Caregiving

---

## Promotion Process

When promoting a hub:

1. Identify recurring conceptual gravity
2. Verify recurrence across time or context
3. Create an empty hub stub using `hub-stub-generator.md`
4. Link existing notes to the new hub
5. Do not populate hub content immediately

Meaning should accumulate after promotion, not during it.

---

## Automation Guidance

Automation may:
- suggest hub promotion
- flag recurring concepts
- generate empty hub stubs

Automation must not:
- promote hubs automatically
- populate hub content
- demote or merge hubs without review

Hub promotion always requires human confirmation.

---

## Relationship to Note Lifecycle

Hub promotion typically occurs after:
- multiple notes have entered dormancy
- patterns become visible retrospectively

Hub promotion is not part of initial processing.

---

## Default Stance

When uncertain:
- do not promote
- allow recurrence to continue
- favor under-tructuring

A missing hub is safer than a premature one.
